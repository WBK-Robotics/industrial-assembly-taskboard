Robotic Assembly Instructions Flexible Components
=================================================

Task 1: Belt
------------

The robot grasps the belt lying on a flat surface next to the Task Board.
The robot pulls the belt over the first of the two pulleys.
The robot then pulls the belt over the second pulley, ensuring that the connection to the first pulley remains intact throughout the process.
Success criteria: The belt must be placed over the two pulleys so that it is centered within the grooves of both pulleys. If this condition is met, the assembly is considered successful.

Task 2: Chain
-------------

The robot grasps the chain lying on a flat surface next to the Task Board.
The robot connects the chain to the first sprocket.
The robot then pulls the remaining part of the chain over the second sprocket, ensuring that the connection to the first sprocket remains intact.
Finally, the robot adjusts the chain tensioner in the middle of the assembly component to apply proper tension to the chain.
Success criteria: The chain must be placed on both sprockets, and the chain tensioner must be rotated until the chain is under sufficient tension. Once proper tension is achieved, the assembly is considered successful.

Task 3: Pneumatic Tubing
------------------------

The robot grasps the pneumatic tubing lying on a flat surface next to the Task Board.
The robot pushes one end of the tubing into the first pneumatic connection fitting mounted on the Task Board.
The robot then routes the other end of the tubing through the first tubing guide located on the opposite side of the Taskboard.
Next, the robot guides the tubing through a 180-degree turn using the adjacent routing part.
After that, the robot routes the tubing through a 90-degree turn using the third tubing guide.
Finally, the robot inserts the second end of the tubing into the second pneumatic connection fitting mounted on the Taskboard.
Success criteria: The tubing must be routed as shown in Figure 2, passing through all tubing guides in the correct sequence. Both ends must be securely inserted into the pneumatic connectors so that they cannot be removed by light pulling.
